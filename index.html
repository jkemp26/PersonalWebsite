About Me

Hi! My name is Jordan Kemp and I am currently enjoying my third year studying Mechanical Engineering at Virginia Tech. I'm from Farmingdale, New Jersey and I have always been interested in understanding how systems work. I'm passionate about studying propulsion systems, and pushing the boundaries of engineering.

If any of this sounds intersting to you, feel free to reach out to me!


Undergraduate Research

I conduct undergraduate research in the Bioelectromechanical Systems Laboratory (BEMS). We study a process called 'Irreverible Electroporation', a non-thermal ablation cancer treatment technique that uses electric fields to induce apoptic cell death. My research in Phase Changing Materials (PCMs) has been published in Oxford Abstracts.

Trane

This past summer, I worked as a Manufacturing Engineering Intern in the Residential Division of Trane, a company that designs and manufacturers HVAC systems. I was a part of the 3P Transition Team, desiging the manufacturing process for the new S8 furnace model family. I also applied Lean Methodologies to remove waste from the current processes.

Baja SAE Design Team

I am a member of the Baja SAE Design Team, an undergraduate-led team tasked to design and build an off-road vehicle that can survive rough terrain. I work on the Rear H-Arm Suspension Design Sub-team, using SolidWorks and CORTIME to optimize kinematics and weight distribution to ensure proper load distribution.

Dean's Team

For the past two years, I have been a member of the College of Engineering Dean's Team, a prestigious group of highly motivated, dedicated, and spirited undergraduate engineering students selected by the Associate Dean for Academic Affairs. On this team, I am an ambassador for the College, assisting recruiting efforts via informational sessions for prospective students and serving on student panels.

I like to discuss new projects and opportunities!

If you are interested in any of my projects or want to chat, please reach out to me!
